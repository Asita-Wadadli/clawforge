This page is for what happens after interest turns into action. Use it before the strategy call or send it to anyone who books so the conversation starts at a useful level instead of from zero.

What happens next

We review the issue, identify the pressure points, and decide whether you need Rapid Response Desk, recurring media support, campaign communication help, or a broader political communication system.

What to bring

Bring the source material, urgency level, response goal, and where the communication will be used.

What you leave with

A clearer recommendation on structure, likely package fit, and the next practical action.

Prepare these before the call

  • the speech, statement, interview, or issue summary
  • the core communication problem you need solved
  • who needs to use the final output
  • how urgent the issue is
  • whether this is one-off response or ongoing pressure
  • your preferred communication formats: radio, social, commentary, spokesperson notes, public brief, or campaign support

Best questions to answer in advance

  • What happened?
  • Why does it matter right now?
  • What audience needs to hear your response?
  • What would a strong outcome look like?
  • Do you need one response asset or an entire communication rhythm?

What the strategy call should produce

  1. clarity on the actual communication problem
  2. clarity on urgency and deployment channels
  3. clarity on the right service path
  4. clarity on what gets built first
