What to send first

These are the fastest inputs for a clean start.

Required first inputs

  • the speech, statement, issue, interview, or controversy summary
  • what happened and why it matters right now
  • how urgent the issue is
  • where the final communication will be used
  • what output you need first

Helpful context

  • your role: campaign, commentator, advocacy group, civic organization, or public-interest media
  • the audience you need to reach
  • any tone or message discipline constraints
  • what has already been said publicly, if anything
  • what you want the audience to understand or do
What happens next
  1. We review the source material and identify the real communication problem.
  2. We determine the strongest response angle and what the audience needs clarified.
  3. We build the first response assets: scripts, briefs, social packs, or talking points.
  4. You review, deploy, record, publish, or brief from a much stronger starting point.

What a good first delivery can include

  • issue summary
  • core claim analysis
  • contradictions or accountability gaps
  • response angle
  • radio-ready script or statement
  • social response pack
  • short public brief if needed
Turnaround expectations

Immediate pressure

If the issue is already moving fast, clearly mark that in your message so the first response can be prioritized.

Same-day / 24-hour issues

Best for active public statements, interviews, controversy cycles, and rapid public-education needs.

Ongoing pressure

If the situation is not a one-off, the work can evolve from response support into a cleaner recurring communication rhythm.
